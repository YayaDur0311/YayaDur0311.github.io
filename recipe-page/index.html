Karak Tea

  • PREP TIME:5 MINUTES
  • TOTAL TIME: 20 MINUTES
  • COOK TIME:15 MINUTES
  • YIELDS: 4


    • Creamy, bold, and deeply spiced, karak tea is the Middle Eastern cousin of chai, known for its rich flavors and comforting warmth. Infused with cardamom, cinnamon, and ginger, it’s simmered slowly to create a velvety, fragrant cup. Perfect for chilly mornings or whenever you need a little extra warmth, this tea never disappoints.

    Jump To Recipie

    Karak tea is the kind of drink that wraps you up in warmth and comfort with every sip. Super popular throughout the Middle East, it’s similar to chai but has its own distinctive flavor profile that’s bold, creamy, and spiced just right. What makes karak tea stand out is the deep richness of the evaporated milk and the aromatic blend of spices like cardamom, cinnamon, and ginger. It’s perfect for when you’re craving something indulgent yet simple to make, whether you’re treating yourself or are having company over. This recipe is all about layering those spices and flavors, and the best part is that you can adjust it to suit your taste.

    Ingredients

    • 2 cups water
    • 2 cups evaporated milk
    • 6 to 8 cardamom pods, lightly crushed
    • 3 cinnamon sticks
    • 1 ginger cube (or a small piece of fresh ginger)
    • 1/4 cup light brown sugar (adjust to taste)
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
    • 4 black/red tea bags

    Instructions

    1. In a medium pot, combine the water, evaporated milk, cardamom pods, cinnamon sticks, and ginger. Bring the mixture to a gentle boil over medium heat.
    2. Once it starts bubbling, reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 5 to 7 minutes. This allows the spices to infuse the milk and water with their rich, warm flavors.
    3. Place the tea bags into the pot and let them steep for 3 to 5 minutes, depending on how strong you like your tea. Stir occasionally to help the tea blend with the spiced milk. You’ll know it’s ready when the color turns a deep, caramel brown. Stir in the brown sugar until it dissolves completely.
    4. Add the vanilla extract and let it simmer for another minute to enhance the flavor.
    5. Remove the pot from the heat and carefully strain the tea into cups to remove the spices and tea bags.
    6. Serve hot and enjoy!
